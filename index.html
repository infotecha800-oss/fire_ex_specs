Operating Guide

The PASS Method

Four standard operating steps for all 8A Fire Safety ABC Dry Chemical extinguishers. Remember: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep.

P

PULL the Pin

Break the tamper seal and pull the safety pin from the handle. This unlocks the operating lever.

A

AIM at Base

Stand 2–3 meters back. Point the nozzle at the base of the fire, not at the flames.

S

SQUEEZE the Lever

Squeeze the handle lever firmly to discharge the agent. Release to stop the flow.

S

SWEEP Side to Side

Move the nozzle slowly side-to-side across the base until the fire is completely out.

Suitable For
  • Class A — Wood, Paper, Cloth, Trash
  • Class B — Flammable Liquids, Grease
  • Class C — Energized Electrical Equipment
Do NOT Use On
  • Combustible metal fires (Class D)
  • Cooking oil fires without proper agent
  • Do not test discharge unnecessarily

Stage 01

Material Sourcing

Monoammonium Phosphate (MAP) agent sourced from certified international suppliers. Cold-rolled steel cylinder blanks procured from ISO-certified local Philippine mills.

Stage 02

Cylinder Fabrication & Testing

Cylinders are formed, welded, and hydraulically tested at 2.1 MPa to confirm structural integrity before any filling. Shot-blasted and powder-coated for corrosion resistance.

Stage 03

Agent Filling & Pressurization

Precise amount of ABC dry chemical agent loaded, then pressurized to 1.2 MPa working pressure. 100% leak tested.

Stage 04

Quality Inspection & Labeling

Each unit receives comprehensive final inspection including pressure gauge calibration, ISO label, production date , and serial number.
