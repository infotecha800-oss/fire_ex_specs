Operating Guide

The PASS Method

Four standard operating steps for all 8A Fire Safety extinguishers. Remember: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep.

1

PULL the Pin

Break the tamper seal and pull the safety pin from the handle. This unlocks the operating lever.

2

AIM at Base

Stand 2–3 meters back. Point the nozzle at the base of the fire, not at the flames.

3

SQUEEZE the Lever

Squeeze the handle lever firmly to discharge the agent. Release to stop the flow.

4

SWEEP Side to Side

Move the nozzle slowly side-to-side across the base until the fire is completely out.

Suitable For
  • Class A — Wood, Paper, Cloth, Trash
  • Class B — Flammable Liquids, Grease
  • Class C — Energized Electrical Equipment
Do NOT Use On
  • Combustible metal fires (Class D)
  • Cooking oil fires without proper agent
  • Do not test discharge unnecessarily

Stage 01

Material Sourcing

Monoammonium Phosphate (MAP) and HFC-236fa agents are sourced from certified international suppliers. Steel and stainless steel cylinder blanks are procured from ISO-certified local mills.

Stage 02

Cylinder Fabrication & Testing

Cylinders are formed, welded, and hydraulically tested at 2.1 MPa to confirm structural integrity before any filling.

Stage 03

Agent Filling & Pressurization

The specified amount of fire suppression agent is loaded precisely, then the unit is pressurized to the working level.

Stage 04

Quality Inspection & Labeling

Each unit receives comprehensive final inspection including pressure gauge calibration, ISO label, date stamp, and serial number engraving.

Stage 05

Distribution & Post-Sale Service

Units are carefully packaged and distributed from our Quezon City facility. Post-sale recharge and recertification services available nationwide.
