Operating Guide

The PASS Method

Four standard operating steps for all 8A Fire Safety HFC-236fa clean agent extinguishers. Remember: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep.

P

PULL the Pin

Break the tamper seal and pull the safety pin from the handle. This unlocks the operating lever.

A

AIM at Base

Stand 2–3 meters back. Point the nozzle at the base of the fire, not at the flames.

S

SQUEEZE the Lever

Squeeze the handle lever firmly to discharge the agent. Release to stop the flow.

S

SWEEP Side to Side

Move the nozzle slowly side-to-side across the base until the fire is completely out.

  • Class B — Flammable Liquids, Solvents, Grease
  • Class C — Energized Electrical Equipment
  • Sensitive Electronics — No residue damage
Do NOT Use On
  • Class A fires (wood, paper, cloth)
  • Combustible metal fires (Class D)

Every 8A Fire Safety HFC-236fa clean agent extinguisher undergoes a rigorous five-stage production process with quality checks at each stage.

Stage 01

Material Sourcing

HFC-236fa (Hexafluoropropane) gaseous agent sourced from certified international suppliers. Cold-rolled steel cylinder blanks procured from ISO-certified local Philippine mills.

Stage 02

Cylinder Fabrication & Testing

Cylinders are formed, welded, and hydraulically tested at 2.75 MPa to confirm structural integrity before any filling. Shot-blasted and powder-coated for corrosion resistance.

Stage 03

Agent Filling & Pressurization

Precise quantity of HFC-236fa clean agent loaded, then pressurized to 1355 (kPA) at 28 °C working pressure at 21°C. 100% leak tested before sealing.

Stage 04

Quality Inspection & Labeling

Each unit receives comprehensive final inspection including pressure gauge calibration, ISO label, production date , and serial number.

Stage 05

Distribution & Post-Sale Service

Units are carefully packaged and distributed. Post-sale recharge and recertification services available nationwide.
